6ASensory processing · Visual depth cuesMonocular vs. binocular

A patient sustains an injury causing complete, permanent loss of vision in the right eye; the left eye is normal. Which depth cue is the patient now unable to use?

  • AInterposition
  • BRetinal (binocular) disparity
  • CRelative size
  • DMotion parallax
A–D to answer

Retinal disparity is a binocular cue — the brain compares the two slightly different retinal images to judge depth. With one eye there's no second image, so it's lost. Interposition, relative size, and motion parallax are all monocular and survive with one eye.

Bank thisOnly two cues are binocular: retinal disparity and convergence. A one-eyed patient loses exactly those two.
